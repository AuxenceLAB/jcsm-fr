Commissioning

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is your response time in the event of a fault?

Response times are set in your contract (distinct urban and rural SLAs). Thanks to OCPP supervision, a share of incidents is resolved remotely without a site visit. For on-site work, our regional technicians have fully equipped service vehicles and parts in stock.

Which charger brands do you maintain?

We work on every brand on the market: ABB, Schneider, EVBox, Alfen, Kempower, Autel, Tritium, Wallbox, Hager, Legrand, DBT-CEV, Circontrol, Delta and many more. Our technicians are trained and certified by each manufacturer, with direct access to genuine parts and proprietary diagnostic tools.

How does remote supervision work?

Our supervision platform uses the OCPP protocol to collect real-time data from every charger: charge status, error codes, energy consumption and availability. Automatic alerts trigger immediate diagnostics. Technicians can restart a charger, update firmware or adjust the configuration remotely.

What is the difference between preventive and corrective maintenance?

Preventive maintenance anticipates failures through scheduled visits (quarterly or biannual): connector inspection, insulation measurement, torque checks, RCD testing, firmware updates and infrared thermography. Corrective maintenance steps in after a failure to restore service. Customers on regular preventive plans see 60% fewer faults and an extended equipment lifespan.

Do you offer maintenance contracts tailored to your fleet?

Every contract is tailored to the fleet configuration: number of chargers, power ratings (AC and/or DC), geographic distribution of sites and criticality level. Together we define the SLAs, the frequency of preventive visits, the scope of work (chargers, cables, main distribution boards, HV/LV transformers) and reporting arrangements. A dedicated contact coordinates every operation.
